About the project

Domestic violence harms many children each year. This in itself is a big problem that has been present for too long. On top of this, the general public usually assumes that the abuse happens only from men on women. The reality is that 1 in 3 of the reported cases are from women on men. Keep in mind that many cases aren’t reported because men who get abused by women are often ridiculed and not taken seriously. This holds men back from coming out with their story to get help. We want to help families in difficult situations by teaching children in a playful way how to react when they notice or suspect their friend is having a hard time (at home). Children that play the game will also get hints on where they can go for help, for example: teachers and supervisors at school or at kindergarten. We developed a RPG game where you and your friends go on a camping trip with school. You play a dwarf, no gender is linked to this character (or to your fellow dwarf friends). At the beginning of the game you notice some strange things happening to your friends while playing hide-and-seek . By talking to your friends you find out it’s one of your friends causing trouble. You talk to him and find out that your friend has trouble at home. Throughout the game you help your friend to resolve their problems. This report describes the design process we went through to reach our goal.

Reflection

I chose this project, Persuasive Game Design, because I was interested in finding out how to change people’s behaviour in a playful way. My goals for this project were to conduct a user-test with our target group, to be involved with the creative side of the creating of the prototype, and to be the implementer of the team. I wanted to be the implementer because I had been the team leader in my previous project. I had the role of the Creative Director where I made the creative decisions. As an implementer of the team I designed the environment of our final game. During our two user-tests I was able to ask questions to the children on what they thought of the environment, and how they experienced the world that I designed. I used their answers to improve my levels for the final prototype.
